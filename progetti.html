MidAS-Po: Modello idrogeologico delle Acque Sotterranee del distretto idrografico del fiume Po

Scopo del progetto è di aumentare il livello di conoscenza dello stato di salute delle acque sotterranee della pianura padana dal punto di vista qualitativo e quantitativo, in un contesto attuale caratterizzato da frequenti eventi di scarsità idrica. Il sottosuolo della Pianura Padana, che ospita potenti acquiferi nei suoi depositi alluvionali, diventa sempre più una risorsa strategica nella valutazione del bilancio idrico, in ragione della rilevante quota di approvvigionamento idrico che supporta il settore civile, industriale e agricolo.

AMBRA: Assessing alpine and apennine Mountain-front recharge to po plain alluvial aquifers: an essential for supporting sustainaBle management of gRoundwAter resources

The AMBRA project addresses threats to groundwater sustainability in Northern Italy’s Po Plain, a vital drinking water source increasingly stressed by climate change. It focuses on mountain-front recharge (MFR), where Alpine and Apennine water replenishes plains’ aquifers via three pathways: (1) surface MFR (infiltration from mountain-sourced rivers), (2) subsurface flow from mountain bedrock, and (3) local rainfall. Using stable isotopes (δ²H, δ¹⁸O) and chloride-bromide ratios as tracers, combined with stream discharge monitoring, the project quantifies these components via mixing models to reveal recharge dynamics. Two pilot areas—Alpine moraines and Apennine carbonate-alluvial systems—represent diverse geological settings, enabling analysis of how geology and land use affect recharge. A key focus is the role of mountain-derived river water (sMFR) in transporting nitrate pollution from upland agriculture into plains’ groundwater, worsening water quality. By clarifying recharge processes and pollution pathways, AMBRA provides actionable data for Italian authorities to protect critical recharge zones, regulate extraction, and mitigate contamination. Its methods and insights are scalable to other regions reliant on mountain-front aquifers, offering a global framework for balancing water security and climate resilience. The project highlights the urgent need to integrate mountain and plains hydrology in sustainable groundwater management.
